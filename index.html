Research Experience
  • An Adaptive Access Scheme Design for IoT Based on Deep Reinforcement Learning
    October 2019 - June 2020
    In the massive IoT scenario, a large number of IoT devices will concurrently access the network, causing access congestion and large delays. Traditional access solutions cannot meet the access requirements of mIoT scenario. Therefore, we apply reinforcement learning to optimize the access model in LTE-M, controlling the load balancing of multiple base stations, and performed simulations. The average access delay reached approximately 1/3 of the existing LTE-M solution. I completed all the work of modeling, programming and writing with the guidance of Prof. Liu jianqing (UAH) and Prof. Yanru Zhang (UESTC).
  • Interference Identification and Decision Making
    September 2018 - October 2019
    There are varieties of interference during satellite-to-ground communication. By identifying the interference and using a specific link for processing, the communication quality can be effectively improved. I was responsible for applying neural network and group intelligence algorithms for interference identification in this project in National Key Laboratory of Science and Technology on Communications. The final simulation effect was much higher than the requirements of party A.
